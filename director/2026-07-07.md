The System currently has a license for 200 memberships to Advisorator, a weekly technology newsletter written by journalist Jared Newman. Membership also includes access to the Advisorator archives, tech tutorials, and a members-only discussion forum.

So far, memberships have only been open to System and member library staff, but we would like to open the remaining memberships (about 150 of them) to library patrons on a first-come, first-served basis. To that end, I've added information about Advisorator to the [Resources page on our website](https://owwl.org/resources/), along with a link to the sign-up form.

Interested libraries are welcome to promote Advisorator in your library's newsletter (print, email, or both). [This OWWL Docs page](https://docs.owwl.org/Members/Advisorator) includes a Word document with a mini "press release," which includes a link to Resources page/sign-up form mentioned above, and an image of the Advisorator/OWWL logos.

- If your library decides to run a newsletter article, please be sure to use the exact text of the Word document. This ensures that all libraries are communicating the same information to their patrons.
- Use of the logos is optional, but please don't replace them with a different image.

We are asking libraries to hold off on social media promotion for now while we gauge initial interest and monitor the signup process. We'll revisit this once we have a better sense of demand.

I am not sure how quickly the remaining 150 licenses will be claimed. If we do start running low, I will be sure to follow up.

Please feel free to forward this to any staff members who handle your newsletter(s).

Let me know if you have any questions!

Kelsy

## Help with Website Domains

We are exploring a new optional service that would allow member libraries to have their website domain names (i.e., anywherepubliclibrary.org) centrally managed and renewed by the OWWL Library System.

Under this service, OWWL would manage domain registration and annual renewals on behalf of participating libraries, then invoice the library for the cost. The goal is to reduce administrative burden for libraries that lack dedicated IT/tech staff and to prevent service disruptions caused by missed renewals. Domain ownership remains with the library at all times.

We're currently working on program guidelines, including registrar selection, transfer and offboarding procedures, and a formal service agreement. Libraries that express interest now will receive details when the service is ready to launch.

If you're interested in participating when this service becomes available, please open a support ticket with the subject line "Domain Management Interest." Expressing interest is not a commitment. You will have the opportunity to review the service agreement before enrolling.

Questions or feedback about this proposed service can go through a support ticket as well.

## Long-Range Planning Cohort: Save the Date for 2027

The OWWL Library System will host another Long-Range Planning Cohort beginning in 2027, following the same collaborative model used in the 2025 cohort.

The cohort meets on a regular schedule from spring through the following winter, guiding participating libraries through community data collection, internal review, goal setting, and drafting a final long-range plan. I facilitate the meetings and provide guidance and feedback throughout. Libraries that cannot attend live can follow along asynchronously through meeting recordings.

Fifteen slots are available. If your library's current plan expires in 2027 or 2028, or if you're due for a new plan, now is a good time to start a conversation with your board and planning committee about joining. I'd encourage both the Director and the head of the library's planning committee to attend meetings or watch the recordings together.

Full program details, dates, and sign-up information will go out this fall.

## Minimum Standards for Libraries: Policy Development for Public Usage of Library Space and Meeting Rooms

Join us this Friday, July 10, from 2:00 to 3:00 PM online via Zoom, for the final session in the webinar series exploring the new elements in the Minimum Standards for Public and Association Libraries.

As libraries are increasingly referenced as "third places," it's important to have policies in place to guide the use of meeting rooms and other library spaces. Article VI of the Library Bill of Rights states that such facilities should be made available to the public "on an equitable basis, regardless of the beliefs or affiliations of individuals or groups requesting their use." This session covers developing these policies to help library staff and volunteers uphold the First Amendment and the Library Bill of Rights, while keeping access to library spaces fair, equitable, and consistent.

Presented by Erika Jenns, Assistant Director, Director of Librarianship at the Southern Tier Library System, and Brian Hildreth, Executive Director of the Southern Tier Library System.

All webinars are recorded for those unable to attend live. Register and the recording link will be sent automatically.
