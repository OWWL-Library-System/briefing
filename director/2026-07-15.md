## E-Rate Program Faces Elimination Threat

E-Rate is the federal program that provides discounts on internet and telecommunications costs to schools and libraries. All libraries in our system benefit from it through significantly reduced internet costs. 

The FCC has proposed rulemaking that raises the question of whether E-Rate should be terminated. Once the FCC publishes its notice in the Federal Register, expected in the next couple of weeks, a public comment period will open, though exact deadlines are not yet set.

This is a concern for all libraries. The bottom line is that if E-Rate is elimintated, monthly internet bills for libraries will no longer be subsidized by the program; that will mean a ~90% increase in the amount libraries will pay for internet. 

SHLB, a national coalition supporting school and library broadband access, is holding a webinar on the proposal, "The Future of E-Rate: Inside the FCC's Proposal and How to Make Your Voice Heard," Thursday, July 16 at 2:00 PM ET.

I will keep you updated as this develops. In the meantime, you can take action now using the American Library Association's campaign link below. 

[Take Action to Save E-Rate](https://action.ala.org/campaign/saveourerate/)

## Provisional PC Ordering and Pricing Update

New York State has not signed an updated contract with a PC vendor. OWWL has created a provisional computer ordering form using Dell's "NYS SLG Special Pricing" option.

[Provisional PC Order Form](https://docs.owwl.org/pub/Members/MediatedComputerPurchasing/Provisional_PC_Order_Form.pdf)

### Why Prices Are Up

Computer prices have risen sharply, driven largely by demand from AI data centers. Memory prices are up 200 to 400 percent depending on type. Modern hard drives are built using the same memory, so drive prices have risen as well. These increases are expected to continue through 2026, and some experts believe prices will not stabilize for another three to five years.

### Temporary Age Waiver

Because of these high prices, OWWL is temporarily waiving the requirement that computers be six years old or less to receive OWWL support.

### Wireless and Monitors

Desktop computers ordered through this form will not include wireless. If you need wireless, let OWWL know and they can provide an Amazon link for a recommended adapter.

Monitors are not included on this order form, since they are not part of the Dell NYS SLG pricing. If you need monitors, OWWL can request custom quotes from Dell. If your existing monitors still work, consider keeping them and putting the funds toward computers instead.

[Computer Options and Details](https://docs.owwl.org/Members/MediatedComputerPurchasing#Computer_Options)

### Linux LTSP Phase-Out

OWWL is phasing out Linux support. Existing Linux LTSP systems will expire in June 2028. This is due to incompatibility between the current Linux environment and newer Linux versions without a full redesign, along with a drop in the number of Linux sites to about half of the original count. Please plan accordingly.

Questions on any of this? Open a support ticket.

## Four County Library System Joining OverDrive Reciprocal Lending

Four County Library System is joining the OverDrive reciprocal lending agreement currently shared by OWWL Library System, Southern Tier Library System, and Finger Lakes Library System.

Integration is expected to go live end of July to early August. OverDrive is also releasing a Libby update in late July that will streamline finding partner libraries and getting visitor access, which is part of why the exact date is still being finalized.

Nothing changes for your staff or patrons in the meantime. Please share this with your staff so they are aware of the expanded partnership when it takes effect.
