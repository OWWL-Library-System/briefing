## Reminder: Direct Access Plan and Minimum Standards Assurance Submissions

Two separate board actions are still outstanding. Here's a quick reminder of what each one requires and when it's due.

### Direct Access Plan (due July 31, 2026)

Commissioner's Regulation §90.3 requires a majority of member library boards to approve the 2027 to 2031 Direct Access Plan before the OWWL Library System Plan of Service can be submitted to the New York State Education Department.

If your board has not yet voted, place this on the next meeting agenda. The motion language is:

> [TRUSTEE NAME] moves to approve the OWWL Library System 2027 to 2031 Direct Access Plan as a required component of the OWWL Library System 2027 to 2031 Plan of Service, pursuant to Commissioner's Regulation §90.3, which requires approval by a majority of member library boards before the plan may be submitted to the New York State Education Department, and directs the Board President to sign the Direct Access Plan Statement of Agreement and to forward a copy of these minutes and the agreement form to the OWWL Library System Executive Director. [TRUSTEE NAME] seconds, and the motion passed unanimously.

Once your board has voted, send Ron a copy of the minutes and the signed [Statement of Agreement](https://docs.owwl.org/pub/Members/DirectorBriefing/Direct%20Access%20Plan%20Statement%20of%20Agreement.pdf?_t=1779809211).

### Minimum Standards Assurance (due August 2026)

Commissioner's Regulation §90.2 requires every library to confirm to the System that it is in compliance with the New York State Minimum Standards for Public and Association Libraries. A convenient time to bring this to your board is alongside your annual report assurances, since it's a similar type of motion. If your library did not make that motion then, please do so at your next board meeting. Without all libraries completing this action, it holds of LLSA funding for our entire System. 

The motion language is:

> [TRUSTEE NAME] moves that the [Library Name] Board of Trustees affirms that the library is in full compliance with the New York State Minimum Standards for Public and Association Libraries, as outlined in Commissioner's Regulation §90.2, and directs [NAME] to email the OWWL Library System Executive Director a link to the board meeting minutes once posted to the library's website, within two weeks of the meeting, in accordance with New York State Open Meetings Law. [TRUSTEE NAME] seconds, and the motion passed unanimously.

As part of the same Minimum Standards update, all required written policies (selection and reconsideration, meeting room and space use, code of conduct, confidentiality of records, personnel, disaster preparedness, and financial controls) must be in place by January 1, 2027. Sample policies are available in the [Policy Library](https://docs.owwl.org/Community/PolicyLibrary).

Questions on either of these? Reach out to Ron directly.

## Reminder: Provisional PC Ordering and Pricing Update

As a reminder, New York State has still not signed an updated contract with a PC vendor. OWWL has created a provisional computer ordering form using Dell's "NYS SLG Special Pricing" option.

[Provisional PC Order Form](https://docs.owwl.org/pub/Members/MediatedComputerPurchasing/Provisional_PC_Order_Form.pdf)

### Why Prices Are Up

Computer prices have risen sharply, driven largely by demand from AI data centers. Memory prices are up 200 to 400 percent depending on type. Modern hard drives are built using the same memory, so drive prices have risen as well. These increases are expected to continue through 2026, and some experts believe prices will not stabilize for another three to five years.

### Temporary Age Waiver

Because of these high prices, OWWL is temporarily waiving the requirement that computers be six years old or less to receive OWWL support.

### Wireless and Monitors

Desktop computers ordered through this form will not include wireless. If you need wireless, let OWWL know and they can provide an Amazon link for a recommended adapter.

Monitors are not included on this order form, since they are not part of the Dell NYS SLG pricing. If you need monitors, OWWL can request custom quotes from Dell. If your existing monitors still work, consider keeping them and putting the funds toward computers instead.

[Computer Options and Details](https://docs.owwl.org/Members/MediatedComputerPurchasing#Computer_Options)

### Linux LTSP Phase-Out

OWWL is phasing out Linux support. Existing Linux LTSP systems will expire in June 2028. This is due to incompatibility between the current Linux environment and newer Linux versions without a full redesign, along with a drop in the number of Linux sites to about half of the original count. Please plan accordingly.

Questions on any of this? Open a support ticket.
