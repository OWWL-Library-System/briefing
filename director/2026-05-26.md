## Construction Aid

### FY 2027: Intent to Apply Deadline is May 31

The deadline to submit an Intent to Apply for FY 2027 Construction Aid is May 31, 2026. This is the required first step in the application process. Libraries may not begin the full application until their Intent to Apply has been reviewed and approved.

Before you submit, make sure you have the following in place:

- Contractor quotes showing total project cost. Architect estimates are not accepted at this stage.

Contact Ron and Kelly with any questions.

## Mandatory Staff Security Trainings

At its May 13 meeting, the OWWL Library System Board of Trustees passed a resolution requiring all current holders of OWWL email and Evergreen accounts to complete two security training modules by September 30, 2026. The Board also adopted an update to the [Systems Access and Confidentiality of Library Records Policy](https://owwl.org/system/policies/systems-access-and-confidentiality-of-library-records-policy/) that sets this training requirement for new authorized users going forward.

This requirement applies to all staff, directors, and System staff with active accounts. Both courses are available now through the OWWL Staff Academy on Niche Academy.

[The full resolution can be found here](https://docs.owwl.org/pub/Members/DirectorBriefing/Doc%202605-7%20Resolution%20Mandatory%20System%20Access%20and%20Email%20Security%20Training%20for%20Current%20Account%20Holders.pdf?_t=1779809125).

### What directors need to do

- Tell your staff about the trainings and direct them to complete both by September 30.
- Complete both trainings yourself.
- Track completion for your team. A practical approach is to have staff email you their completion certificates and add them to personnel files, the same way you would handle sexual harassment prevention training certificates.

The two trainings are:

- [Email Security](https://my.nicheacademy.com/owwl-staffacademy/course/80582) (approximately 15 minutes)
- [Systems Access and Confidentiality of Library Records](https://my.nicheacademy.com/owwl-staffacademy/course/92699) (approximately 25 minutes)

Staff hired after October 1, 2025 may have already completed the Email Security training as part of onboarding. They still need to complete the Systems Access training if they have not done so, and should send both certificates to you.

### Logging in to Niche Academy

Staff should navigate to [my.nicheacademy.com/owwl-staffacademy](https://my.nicheacademy.com/owwl-staffacademy/) and log in with their @owwl.org email address. If they do not have an account, they can click "Enroll for Free" to register. Staff on shared circulation computers should confirm they are logged into their own account before starting a training.

### What happens if someone misses the deadline

Accounts that have not completed both trainings by September 30, 2026 will have their OWWL email and Evergreen passwords reset. This is a security measure. Access will be suspended until the trainings are completed and confirmed.

Restoring access requires a written request to Ron that includes a justification for missing the deadline, documentation that the account holder has signed the local Systems Access and Confidentiality of Library Records Acknowledgment Form, and confirmation that all current authorized users at the library have been reviewed for compliance. There will be no deadline extensions.

## Direct Access Plan 2027–2031

The OWWL Library System Board of Trustees approved the 2027–2031 Direct Access Plan at its May 13, 2026 meeting. The plan was open for public comment by member library directors and boards in March and April before coming to the Board for approval.

Commissioner's Regulation §90.3 requires that a majority of member library boards approve the Direct Access Plan before the OWWL Library System 2027–2031 Plan of Service can be submitted to the New York State Education Department. Your board's approval is needed.

The plan and the Statement of Agreement form are attached.

### What your board needs to do

Place the Direct Access Plan on the agenda at your board's next meeting and bring it to a vote. The action is straightforward and should not require extended discussion.

The motion language is:

> [TRUSTEE NAME] moves to approve the OWWL Library System 2027–2031 Direct Access Plan as a required component of the OWWL Library System 2027–2031 Plan of Service, pursuant to Commissioner's Regulation §90.3, which requires approval by a majority of member library boards before the plan may be submitted to the New York State Education Department, and directs the Board President to sign the Direct Access Plan Statement of Agreement and to forward a copy of these minutes and the agreement form to the OWWL Library System Executive Director. [TRUSTEE NAME] seconds, and the motion passed unanimously.

### What to return to Ron by July 31, 2026

Once your board has voted, two things need to come back to Ron:

- A copy of the minutes reflecting the motion, emailed directly to Ron.
- The signed [Statement of Agreement](https://docs.owwl.org/pub/Members/DirectorBriefing/Direct%20Access%20Plan%20Statement%20of%20Agreement.pdf?_t=1779809211), completed and signed by your board president.

The Statement of Agreement is a separate regulatory requirement under Commissioner's Regulation §90.3. The attached form takes about two minutes to complete.

## Love Your Library Funding Assurance

The OWWL Library System has received designated Love Your Library State Aid for Summer Reading. Member libraries that plan to offer Summer Reading programming in 2026 are eligible for a share of these funds.

The deadline to return the assurance form is this Friday, May 29. Twenty-eight libraries have responded so far.

To receive funds, your library needs to complete and return the attached 2026 Love Your Library Funds Assurance form confirming that the library will provide Summer Reading programming this year. Each library's share depends on the number of eligible libraries, with a minimum of $64.

Funds may be used for:

- Purchased services in support of Summer Reading, including contracts for planning, outreach, promotion, presenters, performers, training, stipends for staff training, partnership projects, publications, and evaluators.
- Supplies and materials in support of Summer Reading, including print, audio, and e-resource library materials for ages birth to 21, program-related supplies, and equipment or computers and devices costing less than $5,000.

Full guidelines are available at [nysl.nysed.gov/libdev/lylplate/guidelines](https://nysl.nysed.gov/libdev/lylplate/guidelines).

Return the completed form to Piety at [pexley@owwl.org](mailto:pexley@owwl.org) by May 29. Questions can also go to Piety.

## Board Meeting Rundown: May 13, 2026

The OWWL Library System Board of Trustees held its regular meeting on May 13, 2026. The Directors Report is attached.

### Policy Updates

The Board approved updates to two existing policies and adopted one new policy.

**[Computer and Technology Support Policy](https://owwl.org/system/policies/computer-support-policy/)** was amended to add a Windows Update Compliance section. Member libraries must keep enrolled computers powered on and connected to the network at regular intervals to receive updates. Computers that have not received updates within 60 days of the current patch level will be considered out of compliance. OWWL Library System is authorized to disable network or system access for out-of-compliance devices until updates are applied, with advance notice to libraries whenever possible.

**[Systems Access and Confidentiality of Library Records Policy](https://owwl.org/system/policies/systems-access-and-confidentiality-of-library-records-policy/)** was amended to add a Training Compliance and Credential Security section. The amendment establishes a 30-day window for newly issued account holders to complete required training, with password resets applied to accounts that fall out of compliance. It also codifies the process for restoring access, including the requirement for a written request from the library director to the Executive Director and a response timeline of ten business days. This amendment is connected to the resolution on required security trainings covered in the Mandatory Staff Security Trainings article above.

**[Name, Branding, and Identity Use Policy](https://owwl.org/system/policies/name-branding-itendity-use-policy/)** is a new policy establishing guidelines for authorized use of the OWWL Library System name, branding, logos, and related assets. The policy defines the OWWL name as a protected organizational identifier rather than a geographic descriptor, prohibits unauthorized use by any individual, group, or entity, and requires a formal affiliate agreement for any external use. It specifies which logos member libraries may use without prior permission and which require approval, and addresses use of the OWWL name in social media accounts and the shared catalog URL. This policy replaces the former System Branding, Logos, and Assets Usage Policy, which has been revoked.

### Other Board Actions

The Board approved the 2027–2031 Direct Access Plan, discussed in the Direct Access Plan article above.

The Board passed the Resolution on Required Systems Access and Email Security Training for Current Account Holders, also discussed above.
